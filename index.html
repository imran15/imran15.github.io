Welcome


Short bio: My full name is "Muhammad Imran", but you can call me "Imran". I work as a Postdoctoral researcher in the Social Computing group at the Qatar Computing Research Institute (QCRI). Prior to QCRI, I was a PhD candidate at the University of Trento, where I received my PhD degree in Computer Science on March 2013. My current research interests include Social Computing, Crisis Computing, Crowdsourcing, and Web Engineering research areas. Combination of my research interests can be seen live at AIDR (Artificial Intelligence for Disaster Response) project. During my PhD, I also taught two courses related to the Web Service Design and Web Engineering areas in the University of Trento from 2009-2012.

I received a Master of Science in Computer Science degree with a distinction from Mohammad Ali Jinnah University, Islamabad, Pakistan in 2007. I'm a Oracle Certified Professional (OCP) and I obtained my OCP certification in 2004 from the Oracle Corporation, California, USA.

[Nov, 2013] - AIDR platform launched
I feel proud to announce that our team at QCRI has successfully launched AIDR(Artificial Intelligence for Disaster Response) platform at the ICCM-2013 conference. AIDR is a free, open-source, and easy-to-use platform to filter and classify relevant microblog messages during humanitarian crises. AIDR uses human intelligence and machine intelligence to collect and automatically tag up to thousands of messages per minute...more
